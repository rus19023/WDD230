Fish Haven - Gateway to Yellowstone

March 21, 2020
Next to beautiful, fish-paradise Bear Lake, Fish Haven sat as a little "wide spot in the road" on US Route 89. Tourists passed through on their trips to Yellowstone National Park quite often. Back in 2000, there was one business there, Glady's Place, that sold everything from videos to food to lottery tickets, like a one-stop mall! Since then, paradise has grown, but the natural beauty of the area will be there forever. Bear Lake has been called the "Caribbean of the Rockies" due to the gorgeous turquoise water.

Did we mention the fish? Bonneville cisco (Prosopium gemmifer), Bonneville whitefish (Prosopium spilonotus), Bear Lake whitefish (Prosopium abyssicola), and Bear Lake sculpin (Cottus extensus) are the varieties endemic to Bear Lake. Cutthroat trout season is coming up soon! It is no longer on the endemic list, but is in season from April 1st to the middle of June. There are rental cabins and other related businesses nearby to accomodate any fisher-person, or anyone who just wants to drink in nature.
